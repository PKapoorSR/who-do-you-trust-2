Biochemistry degree from the University of Western Ontario. Competed in powerlifting at the provincial and national level. YouTube channel active since 2014. Every video cites peer-reviewed research on screen. He has publicly reversed positions when the evidence changed — documented across his channel history.

Hovland, Janis & Kelley (1953) — Expertise, Trustworthiness, Appeal
Expertise

Jeff's perceived expertise closely matches his actual credentials — which is unusual in fitness media. A biochemistry degree, competition history, and a decade of citing sources on screen all function as professional self-disclosure (PFSD), which research shows boosts perceived expertise (Zhang & Lu, 2023). Expertise here is both real and performed.

Trustworthiness

He discloses all brand partnerships and has publicly turned down brands he does not personally use. Reversing positions when evidence changed signals honesty over ego — the kind of behavior that sustains long-term credibility. The MacroFactor app creates a real conflict of interest (he co-created it and earns referral income), but that conflict is disclosed.

Appeal / PSI

Lower parasocial connection than the other three creators. The audience relationship is professional rather than personal — viewers follow for content accuracy, not personality. This makes Jeff the only creator here whose credibility does not depend on the audience liking him.

Hovland, Janis & Kelley (1953) — Expertise, Trustworthiness, Appeal
Expertise

No credentials or professional background. Authority is entirely personal experience — "this happened to me" is the whole argument. This is personal self-disclosure (PSD), which research shows can reduce perceived expertise (Zhang & Lu, 2023). His audience does not follow him because he knows things. They follow him because they trust him personally.

Trustworthiness

Radical disclosure of personal incidents — the overdose, failed cycles, gambling losses — creates a strong sense of honesty. But disclosure of personal drama and disclosure of financial conflicts are separate things. The Roobet crypto casino partnership is inconsistently disclosed, often embedded inside candid personal content where it is least visible as advertising.

Appeal / PSI

The highest parasocial connection of the four creators. His audience forms a one-sided personal relationship with Shane — comments read like conversations with a friend, not reactions to content. This is the primary credibility mechanism: trust flows from the relationship, not from evidence. PSI makes audiences extend more trust than they would to a stranger with the same track record.

Mike Tornabene is an NYU graduate and professional comedian who has played the character Dom Mazzetti since December 2012. Dom is a fictional gym bro who gives confidently wrong advice. Hovland, Janis & Kelley (1953) — Expertise, Trustworthiness, Appeal
Expertise

Dom Mazzetti's expertise is deliberately performed and wrong by design — that is the joke. Mike Tornabene is an NYU graduate and comedian, not a fitness professional. The satire format openly signals that it is not providing accurate fitness advice, which is itself a form of intellectual honesty.

Trustworthiness

Satire signals the creator is not claiming to be right — which functions as a kind of disclosure. The primary trustworthiness concern is brand integration: the David Protein sponsorship arrives inside comedy bits rather than clearly labeled ad segments. You don't apply skepticism to things you're laughing at.

Appeal / PSI

High comedic appeal creates pre-trust before any claim lands. The audience feels like insiders who share the cultural knowledge to get the joke — which is a form of parasocial identity. The content validates: I'm self-aware enough to find this funny, which is itself an identity, not just entertainment.

Hovland, Janis & Kelley (1953) — Expertise, Trustworthiness, Appeal
Expertise

No verifiable credentials. Concepts like "energy alignment," "frequency" and "cosmic timing" have no scientific basis as fitness coaching tools. The claims are not designed to be verified — they operate on spiritual authority rather than empirical evidence. This is a category of perceived expertise with no connection to actual qualifications.

Trustworthiness

Very limited public information about JPG's identity. Revenue sources are unclear. The calm, certain-sounding aesthetic performs trustworthiness without the disclosures that would make it verifiable. Absence of disclosed conflicts does not mean absence of conflicts — it may simply reflect limited transparency.

Appeal / PSI

Calming, certain-sounding delivery creates appeal through comfort rather than evidence. Discovered primarily through the TikTok algorithm (FYP) rather than active searching — what Barnette (2022) calls technomancy: the algorithm creates the feeling of content arriving at the right moment, which reinforces the spiritual framing.
